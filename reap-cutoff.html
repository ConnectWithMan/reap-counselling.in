REAP Cutoff 2026 Overview

The REAP Cutoff represents the minimum rank (for JEE Main candidates) or percentage (for Class 12th candidates) required to secure admission in a specific college and branch. The Centre for Electronic Governance (CEG) releases the opening and closing ranks after each round of the REAP Seat Allotment.

Candidates must note that there are separate merit lists:

  • JEE Main Basis: Priority is given to JEE Main scores. The cutoff is defined in terms of All India Rank (AIR).
  • Class 12th Basis: For seats remaining vacant after exhausting the JEE Main merit list, admissions are based on 12th percentile.

Factors Affecting REAP Cutoff

The REAP 2026 Cutoff is not fixed and changes every year. Several factors influence the opening and closing ranks:

  • Number of Applicants: Higher number of registrations usually leads to higher cutoffs (lower ranks).
  • Difficulty Level of JEE Main: If the exam is tough, cutoffs might drop.
  • Seat Availability: The total number of seats in a specific branch and college.
  • Category Reservation: Cutoffs are lower for SC, ST, OBC, MBC, and EWS categories compared to General.
  • Previous Year Trends: Past data often sets the benchmark for the current year.
How to Check REAP Cutoff?

Once the counselling rounds begin, candidates can check the official cutoff by following these steps:

  • Step 1: Visit the official REAP website (reap2026.in or ceg.rajasthan.gov.in).
  • Step 2: Look for the "Merit List" or "Opening & Closing Rank" link on the homepage.
  • Step 3: Select the Round Number (Round 1, 2, or 3).
  • Step 4: Download the PDF file.
  • Step 5: Search for your desired college and branch to view the cutoff rank.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. What is the expected cutoff for MBM Jodhpur CSE?

For General category students, the expected cutoff for CSE in MBM Jodhpur is around 45,000 to 55,000 JEE Main Rank.

2. Does REAP have a separate cutoff for Class 12th students?

Yes, seats are first filled via JEE Main merit. Remaining seats are filled via Class 12th percentile, which has its own separate cutoff list.

3. When will the REAP 2026 Cutoff be released?

The cutoff is typically released in July or August 2026, following the conclusion of the first round of counselling.

4. What is the difference between Opening and Closing Rank?

Opening Rank is the rank of the first student admitted to a branch, while Closing Rank is the rank of the last student admitted. The Closing Rank is essentially the cutoff.

5. Where can I check the official cutoff?

You can check the official cutoff on the REAP website (ceg.rajasthan.gov.in) under the "Merit List" section.

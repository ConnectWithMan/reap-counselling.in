Document Verification Process

Document verification is a crucial step in the Rajasthan Engineering Admission Process (REAP). Candidates must upload scanned copies of their original documents during the online registration and choice-filling phase. Additionally, physical verification of these documents is required when reporting to the allotted institute.

Failure to produce the required documents in their original format, or submitting false/invalid certificates, will result in the immediate cancellation of the candidate's allotted seat.

List of Required Documents

Candidates are advised to keep the following documents ready before starting their REAP 2026 registration:

Essential Documents (For All Candidates)

  • Aadhar Card: Valid Aadhar card or Aadhar acknowledgment receipt.
  • 10th Marksheet & Certificate: Used as proof of Date of Birth.
  • 12th (10+2) Marksheet: Indicating marks obtained in Physics, Mathematics, and other subjects.
  • JEE Main 2026 Scorecard: If applying on the basis of JEE Main rank.
  • Passport Size Photographs: Recent color passport-size photographs.
  • Medical Fitness Certificate: As per the prescribed format by REAP authorities.
  • Fee Receipt: Proof of payment for REAP registration/counselling fee.

Category & Specific Documents (If Applicable)

  • Domicile Certificate: Required for candidates claiming Rajasthan state quota.
  • Category Certificate: SC/ST/OBC (Non-Creamy Layer)/MBC/EWS certificate issued by a competent authority. (Note: OBC/MBC/EWS certificates must be recent as per government norms).
  • Income Certificate: Required for candidates applying under the Tuition Fee Waiver Scheme (TFWS).
  • Ex-Servicemen (EXS) / PwD Certificate: For candidates claiming reservation under these specific categories.
  • Kashmiri Migrants (KM) Certificate: Issued by the competent authority, if applicable.
Important Instructions for Uploading

Document Format & Size

Ensure that all scanned documents meet the specified criteria to avoid rejection during the online verification process:

  • Documents should generally be in JPG, JPEG, or PDF format.
  • The file size typically must be between 50 KB to 200 KB (check the official brochure for exact sizes).
  • Scans must be clear, legible, and taken directly from the original documents (not from photocopies).

Always carry at least two sets of self-attested photocopies along with your original documents when reporting to the allotted college!

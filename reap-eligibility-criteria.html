REAP Eligibility Criteria 2026

Understanding the REAP Eligibility Criteria 2026 is the first step for any aspirant wishing to secure a seat in Rajasthan's engineering colleges. The Centre for Electronic Governance (CEG) strictly adheres to AICTE guidelines. Candidates must ensure they meet these standards before participating in the REAP Choice Filling Process.

1. B.E. / B.Tech Educational Qualification

For admission into the first year of Bachelor of Technology courses, the candidate must meet the following academic standards:

  • Qualifying Examination: The candidate must have passed the 10+2 (Class XII) examination from a recognized board (RBSE/CBSE/ICSE or equivalent).
  • Compulsory Subjects: Physics and Mathematics are mandatory.
  • Optional Subjects (Any one): Chemistry, Biotechnology, Biology, Technical Vocational subject, Computer Science, Information Technology, Informatics Practices, Agriculture, Engineering Graphics, or Business Studies.
  • Minimum Percentage:
    • General Category: At least 45% marks aggregate in the above three subjects combined.
    • Reserved Category (SC/ST/Non-Creamy Layer OBC/Non-Creamy Layer MBC of Rajasthan): At least 40% marks aggregate.
  • Diploma Holders: Candidates who have passed a Diploma in Engineering and Technology with at least 45% marks (40% for reserved category) are also eligible, subject to vacancies.

2. B.Arch Eligibility Criteria

Architecture aspirants must adhere to Council of Architecture (CoA) norms:

  • Academic Requirement: Passed 10+2 scheme of examination with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics.
  • Aggregate Marks: Minimum 50% marks in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics and also 50% marks in aggregate of the 10+2 examination.
  • Diploma Holders: 10+3 Diploma Examination with Mathematics as a compulsory subject with at least 50% marks in aggregate.
  • NATA Score: A valid NATA score is mandatory. REAP does not conduct a separate entrance exam for B.Arch; admissions are based on NATA/JEE Paper 2 scores.
Relaxation in Eligibility

The REAP Eligibility Criteria provides specific relaxations for candidates belonging to Rajasthan State domicile who fall under reserved categories.

Category Minimum Marks Required (12th/Diploma)
General / EWS / ORE (Out of Rajasthan) 45%
SC / ST (Rajasthan Domicile) 40%
OBC - NCL (Rajasthan Domicile) 40%
MBC - NCL (Rajasthan Domicile) 40%
PWD (Person with Disability) 40%

Note: Candidates from states other than Rajasthan are considered under the General Category for eligibility purposes, regardless of their category in their home state.

Age Limit & Domicile Rules

REAP Age Limit 2026

One of the most common queries regarding the REAP 2026 Qualification is about the age limit.

  • Lower Age Limit: Candidates should generally be at least 17 years old at the time of admission, though this varies by institute.
  • Upper Age Limit: There is no upper age limit for applying to REAP for B.E./B.Tech courses.

Domicile Criteria for State Quota

To claim seats under the Rajasthan State Quota (85% of total seats), candidates must fulfill the domicile criteria. This is crucial for the REAP Seat Allotment process.

  • Category A: The candidate is a bona fide resident of Rajasthan.
  • Category B: The candidate has studied in Rajasthan for at least 3 years during their schooling (including the qualifying exam).
  • Category C: Wards of serving/retired employees of the Government of Rajasthan.
Documents & Medical Fitness

During the reporting phase, candidates must produce original documents to prove they meet the REAP Eligibility Criteria. Failure to do so may result in the cancellation of the allotted seat.

  • Aadhar Card / ID Proof.
  • 10th Class Marksheet (Date of Birth Proof).
  • 12th Class Marksheet / Diploma Certificate.
  • JEE Main 2026 Score Card (if applicable).
  • Domicile Certificate (for Rajasthan candidates).
  • Category Certificate (SC/ST/OBC/MBC/EWS) issued by competent authority.
  • Medical Fitness Certificate (signed by a Medical Officer).
  • Income Certificate (for TFWS seats).

Important Note

Candidates are advised to ensure they fulfill all eligibility conditions before filling the application form. The fee once paid is non-refundable.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. Is JEE Main compulsory for REAP 2026?

No, JEE Main is not compulsory. However, priority in seat allotment is given to candidates with a valid JEE Main score. If seats remain vacant, they are filled based on Class 12th percentile.

2. What is the minimum percentage required for REAP?

General category candidates need a minimum of 45% aggregate in Physics, Math, and one optional subject. Reserved category candidates from Rajasthan need 40%.

3. Can outside Rajasthan candidates apply for REAP?

Yes, candidates from other states can apply under the "Out of Rajasthan" (ORE) quota, which constitutes 15% of the seats in private institutes.

4. Is there any age limit for REAP B.Tech admission?

There is no upper age limit for B.Tech admissions through REAP 2026.

5. What if I have a gap in my studies?

Gap years are accepted, but you may need to provide a Gap Certificate (Affidavit) at the time of reporting to the allotted college.
